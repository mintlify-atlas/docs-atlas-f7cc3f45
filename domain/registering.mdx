## Why a custom domain is recommended

After you deploy to Vercel, your proxy is accessible at a `*.vercel.app` URL. In some regions — particularly mainland China — Vercel's default domains can be affected by DNS pollution, making the proxy unreliable or completely unreachable for your users. Attaching a custom domain avoids this problem because you control the DNS records, and the domain resolves independently of Vercel's own infrastructure.

Even outside regions with DNS issues, a custom domain gives your proxy a stable, branded address that does not change across redeployments.

## Choose a registrar

Several popular registrars offer competitive pricing, especially for first-year registrations:

| Registrar | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| [Namecheap](https://www.namecheap.com) | Affordable pricing, straightforward DNS management panel |
| [GoDaddy](https://www.godaddy.com) | Large selection of TLDs, widely recognized |
| [Alibaba Cloud (Aliyun)](https://www.alibabacloud.com/domain) | Good option for users in mainland China; supports CN domains |

Beginners can typically register a `.com`, `.net`, or `.xyz` domain for well under $10 for the first year. Renewal pricing is usually higher, so check the renewal cost before committing.

<Tip>
  You only need one domain to serve multiple proxied services. Use subdomains — for example, `google.yourdomain.com`, `maps.yourdomain.com` — to point different Vercel projects to the same root domain. Each subdomain gets its own CNAME and TXT record in your DNS panel.
</Tip>

## Register your domain

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a registrar">
    Pick a registrar from the table above or use one you already have an account with. Create an account if you do not have one.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Search for a domain">
    Use the registrar's search tool to find an available domain name. The examples in this documentation use the pattern `google.yourdomain.com`, where `yourdomain.com` is your registered domain and `google` is a subdomain you configure later.

    Choose a short, memorable domain name. The exact name does not affect proxy functionality.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete purchase and verification">
    Add the domain to your cart and complete checkout. After purchase, some registrars — particularly for `.cn` domains or users in mainland China — require real-name verification (实名认证) before the domain becomes active. Follow any verification prompts in your registrar's dashboard.

    Once the domain is active, you are ready to configure DNS records for your Vercel deployment.
  </Step>
</Steps>
