## Required DNS records

You will need to create two DNS records at your registrar:

| Type | Name | Value |
|------|------|-------|
| TXT | `@` or your subdomain | Verification value provided by Vercel |
| CNAME | Your subdomain (e.g., `google`) | `cname.vercel-dns.com` |

<Note>
  If you are adding a root domain (e.g., `yourdomain.com`), use `@` as the record name for the TXT verification entry. If you are adding a subdomain (e.g., `google.yourdomain.com`), use the subdomain label — `google` — as the name for both records. Some registrars require you to enter the full subdomain; others only the label before the root domain. Check your registrar's documentation if you are unsure.
</Note>

## Configure your domain

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add the domain in Vercel">
    Open your project in the [Vercel dashboard](https://vercel.com/dashboard). Go to **Settings → Domains**. Enter your domain or subdomain (e.g., `google.yourdomain.com`) in the input field and click **Add**.

    Vercel will display the DNS records you need to create.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Copy the TXT verification value">
    On the Domains page, Vercel shows a TXT record with a unique verification value. Copy this value exactly — it is a long string that proves domain ownership. Do not close this page; you will need the CNAME value from here as well.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add the TXT record at your registrar">
    Log in to your domain registrar's DNS management panel. Create a new TXT record:

    - **Type**: `TXT`
    - **Name**: `@` for a root domain, or your subdomain label (e.g., `google`) for a subdomain
    - **Value**: The verification string copied from Vercel

    Save the record.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add the CNAME record">
    In the same DNS panel, create a CNAME record:

    - **Type**: `CNAME`
    - **Name**: Your subdomain label (e.g., `google`)
    - **Value**: `cname.vercel-dns.com`

    Save the record.

    ```
    google.yourdomain.com  →  CNAME  →  cname.vercel-dns.com
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for DNS propagation">
    DNS changes are not instant. Changes can take anywhere from a few minutes to 48 hours to propagate globally, depending on your registrar and DNS TTL settings.

    <Warning>
      Do not assume your domain is misconfigured if it does not resolve immediately. Wait at least 30 minutes before troubleshooting. Full propagation can take up to 24–48 hours in rare cases.
    </Warning>

    You can check propagation progress at a tool such as [dnschecker.org](https://dnschecker.org) by searching for your domain and selecting the TXT or CNAME record type.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify the domain is active in Vercel">
    Return to **Settings → Domains** in your Vercel project. Once Vercel detects the correct DNS records, the domain status changes from **Invalid Configuration** to a green **Valid Configuration** indicator.

    <Info>
      Vercel automatically provisions a TLS certificate for your custom domain once verification succeeds. Your proxy will be accessible over HTTPS without any additional configuration on your part.
    </Info>

    Your proxy is now reachable at your custom domain. Requests to `google.yourdomain.com` are routed through Vercel to the target URL configured in `api/proxy.js`.
  </Step>
</Steps>
